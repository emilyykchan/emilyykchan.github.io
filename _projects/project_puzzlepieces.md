## Overview

A contactless tangram puzzle game designed for children with autism (ASD), developed in collaboration with La Cañada High School in Valencia. The project explores how gesture interaction can support learning through play.

## Interaction

Instead of using a mouse or touchscreen, players interact entirely through hand gestures using a Leap Motion controller. Physical movement is translated into on-screen actions, creating a more engaging and embodied way to play.

## Gameplay

The game is built around a simple tangram mechanic, with increasing difficulty and two complementary modes: one based on visual guidance, and one based on memory.

## Design

The interface was designed to be clear and supportive, with strong colour contrast and minimal distractions. Small interaction details, such as snapping pieces into place, were introduced to reduce frustration and improve usability.

## Outcome

Tested in a real classroom setting, the system was well received by both children and teachers. The gesture-based interaction proved engaging, while the structured gameplay supported learning objectives.
